Franz Kafka

Novelist and writer

Franz Kafka (3 July 1883 – 3 June 1924) was a German-language novelist and writer from Prague. He is widely regarded as a major figure of 20th-century literature. His work fuses elements of realism and the fantastic. It typically features isolated protagonists facing bizarre or surrealistic predicaments and incomprehensible socio-bureaucratic powers. It has been interpreted as exploring themes of alienation, existential anxiety, guilt, and absurdity.

The Metamorphosis
Metamorphosis tells the story of salesman Gregor Samsa, who wakes one morning to find himself inexplicably transformed into a huge insect. It is a seminal work in exploring themes of identity, alienation, and existential dread.
The Castle
The Castle features a protagonist known only as "K." who arrives in a village and struggles to gain access to the mysterious authorities that govern it from a distant, foreboding castle. The novel delves into themes of bureaucracy, isolation, and the human search for meaning.
Letters to Milena
This is a collection of Kafka's letters to Milena Jesenská, written between 1920 and 1923. The letters reveal Kafka's deep thoughts on love, writing, and personal relationships, offering intimate insights into his inner world.
